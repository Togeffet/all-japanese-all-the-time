I’ve tried my hand at simply blogging. Despite having a fairly time-rich life, putting together original and interesting content on a regular basis proved to be impossible for me. And that’s coming from someone who LOVES to see his own words and enjoys writing. So I know how much effort he’s put into this site. As other people have pointed out, both in this post and in the other, though it perhaps seems like a lot of money to join Silverspoons, when you break it down, it’s not much at all. Perhaps, eventually, as he gathers more students and automates his process, it will become lucrative, but at least the initial waves of SilverSpooners are paying a relatively small amount for a relatively high investment of energy and time. I imagine that the money Khatz makes from this and other projects is just enough to allow him to continue to invest such high proportions of his time and energy into DOING SOMETHING HE CARES ABOUT. As much as it would be nice if just giving everything away for free and living on donations paid enough of the bills to make it possible to continue an endeavor like this indefinitely, in the real world it just doesn’t work that way.

As far as Khatz changing tactics, yes, for years he’s been saying, “You don’t need me to hold your hand. You don’t need me to make the day-to-day decisions for you.” Hell, even recently, when he posted a response to the question “What was a day in the life of Khatz like?” And basically he said, “it doesn’t matter what I did. Just look at adding more Japanese to your own life.” and people were all getting their feelings hurt and stuff, and begging for a “serious” answer to the question. So, I think Khatz has simply embraced the fact that some people don’t want or might even find it impossible to, do this without more direction and encouragement. Sure, there are plenty of us self-starters out there (although in my first few weeks of ajatt, i probably emailed khatz 20 times), who CAN and WILL do this deal on our own, there are a lot of people who will benefit from more guidance.

Can he provide that kind of guidance for free? No, as I spoke about above, it’s unrealistic to think that would even be possible unless he was independently wealthy or something, and to the best of my knowledge that he is not. As others have pointed out in the original post, a course at your local community college, a boring course where you’d probably learn the kana, a handful of words, and a bunch of useless grammar, such a course would cost you MULTIPLE TIMES what SilverSpooners are paying Khatz for this new service. The biggest difference, though, Khatz has GUARANTEED you fluency. I can guarantee you just as assuredly that after two semesters of college Japanese you will NOT be fluent. Not even close. I took 3 years of Japanese at uni and I learned more in two months of AJATT than during all that time.

The difference is, most of you complainers and haters have probably never paid a tuition bill out-of-pocket in your life. You got fin aid, or student loans, or your parents paid for it, so the cost of those college courses doesn’t seem real to you. Meanwhile, this requires “real” money coming out of your own pocket.

Truth-be-told, I don’t have the money for AJATT SS. And honestly, I doubt it’s for me anyways. I like to do things my own way far too much. But for those who can both afford it and benefit from it, I certainly bear no grudge. And I commend Khatz for continuing to pursue something he’s obviously passionate about. I hope the day comes when he never has to take a job or work assignment he doesnt personally want to take again, and that he can make a good living just from doing ajatt stuff, if that’s what he chooses.
