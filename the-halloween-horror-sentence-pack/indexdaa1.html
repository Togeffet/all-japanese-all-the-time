Japan is many things, and one of those things is a great producer and consumer of fear- and thrill-generating experiences.

And you are many things. But above all, you are a lover of Halloween, or as we call it in my country, Christian: هالووين.
What? What, I can’t do impressions of Morgan Freeman as Azeem the Moor in Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves? But it’s not racist if it’s funny and well-intention — oh, it wasn’t funny? OK.

Sorry, that was the American beer commercial Khatzumoto talking. I’m back now. I’m sensitive and caring and I give foot footrubs and use neutral pronouns and “she” a lot when I actually mean “he” because it’s just right.

You like Halloween, because…I dunno…you’re into ancient European harvest festivals, and you love themed holidays; you love celebrating and drinking fruit punch and, gol darn, man — I don’t know what your reasons are!! OK?! I really don’t. But I know that it’s always awesome to be able to inject some Japanese into whatever the world at large is fussing about at the time 😉 .

That’s why 😛 .

Don’t MCDs (Massive-Context Cloze Deletions) Make Sentences Irrelevant? Didn’t The 10,000 Method Sentences Die?

The answer to the first question is no. The answer to the second is yes. MCDs re-orient how we work with sentences; they change how sentence packs are used. And as a result, MCDs actually make sentence packs even more useful by allowing us to absorb and internalize them both more deeply and more effortlessly. “More effortlessly” — that sounds funny, but you get the point! Ultimately, the point is to get the language inside your head, inside your body — to turn the language, as we Japanese say, into part of your “blood and bone” — and MCDs, combined with sentence packs, accomplish this in spectacular fashion.

Sweet! How Can I Get Mine?

The Halloween Horror Sentence Pack (HXP) comes in three nightmarish flavors — nano pumpkin, Wittle Pumpkin and big pimpin’ I mean BIG PUMPKIN. nano pumpkin’s cheapest, wittle pumpkin’s in the middle; BIG PUMPKIN is the most value, born and raised in value village. BIG PUMPKIN is an absolute steal. You choose your poison! All contain and are based on engaging, for-native-by-native subject matter that’s actually fun and funny and interesting and relevant to your life, because how many times are you going to read a textbook that shows you how to say you’re a student with two brothers and a younger sister before you want to shoot yourself and come do some Halloween for real (yeah, apparently Halloween has an ancestor spirit, dead-back-to-life element to it; I didn’t know; I honestly thought it was about drinking and sexy costumes)? Exactly.

Oh, and good luck finding a textbook that talks to you about zombies. That’s right. Zombies and Japanese. Zombies and AJATT. Together. As one. Combined. In the same place. Two headshots with one bullet.
